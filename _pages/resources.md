Here is a list of resources that I found useful in my own research development.
### Praat Scripts:
- [UCLA Praat Script Resources](http://phonetics.linguistics.ucla.edu/facilities/acoustic/praat.html)
- [Praat Vocal Toolkit](https://www.praatvocaltoolkit.com/index.html)
- [SpeCT - The Speech Corpus Toolkit for Praat (Mietta's Praat Scripts)](https://lennes.github.io/spect/)
- [Scripts by Henning Reetz](https://github.com/HenningReetz/Praat-scripts)
- [Scripts by Christian DiCanio](https://www.acsu.buffalo.edu/~cdicanio/scripts.html)
- [Scripts by Matt Winn](http://www.mattwinn.com/praat.html)
- [Scripts by Holger Mitterer](http://www.holgermitterer.eu/research.html)
- [Scripts by Michelle Cohn](https://michelledcohn.com/praat-scripts-2/)
- [Scripts by Jalal Al-Tamimi](https://jalalal-tamimi.github.io/)

### Praat Tutorial:
- [Using Praat for Linguistic Research by Will Styler](https://wstyler.ucsd.edu/praat/)
- [Praat Scripting Resources by Mauricio Figueroa](https://www.mauriciofigueroa.cl/04_scripts/04_scripts.html)
- [Corpus Phonetic Tutorial with Scripting by Eleanor Chadroff](https://eleanorchodroff.com/tutorial/index.html)

### Bilingual Language Assessment:
- [Language Experience and Proficiency Questionnaire](https://bilingualism.northwestern.edu/leapq/)

### Foreign-Accented Speech:
- [International Dialect of English Archive (IDEA)](https://www.dialectsarchive.com/)
- [Speech Accent Archive](https://accent.gmu.edu/)

### Eye-Tracking and Statistics:
- [Eye-Tracking Step-by-Step Tutorial](https://bilingualism.northwestern.edu/eye-tracking-tutorial/)
- [Non-Linear Analysis for Visual World Paradigm](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-59424-8_25)
- [Generalized Additive Mixed Modeling](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.wocn.2018.03.002)

### Statistics and R:
- [R for Linguistics Tutorial by Eleanor Chodroff](https://eleanorchodroff.com/r-for-linguists/)
- [Quantitative Methods for Linguistic Data developed at McGill University](https://people.linguistics.mcgill.ca/~morgan/qmld-book/)
- [Regression Modeling for Linguistic Data by Morgan Sonderegger](https://osf.io/pnumg/)
- [R Tutorial and Notebooks by Jalal Al-Tamimi](https://jalalal-tamimi.github.io/)
- [An Unofficial Statistics Cookbook by Tifani Biro](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tifani-biro_an-unofficial-statistics-cookbook-activity-6958050228637687808-lxRL/): LinkedIn Login Required
- [Statistics Course Archives by Lesa Hoffman at the University of Iowa](https://www.lesahoffman.com/Courses.html): Handouts and videos available for various levels of statistics courses

### Online Experiment:
- [Gorilla Experiment Builder (No coding skills needed)](https://gorilla.sc/): Check if your lab has a subscription! If not, I highly recommend purchasing the promotion offer for 100 tokens after you register. Can be used to collect data in China (as of Sep 2024).
- If you are recruiting Chinese-speaking participants online, consider the WeChat Official Account "线上被试招募"

### Graduate School Application in Psychology, Linguistics, CSD/Speech and Hearing...
- [UConn Magic Initiative](https://magic.initiative.uconn.edu/): I have benefited a lot from this program at UConn. Their website offers genuine advice on finding the right programs & resources and examples on writing graduate application essays. They may also connect you with faculty and students already in the field for individual mentoring. You may find similar opportunities by following them on X.
- [KU Pre-Application Support Service (PASS) Program](https://splh.ku.edu/pre-application-support-service): For applicants in CSD.
- [ASHA Ed Finder](https://find.asha.org/ed/#sort=relevancy): A list of programs at all levels (bachelor's, master's, and doctoral) in CSD in the US with information regarding admissions rate and funding opportunities. This might be the first step of your application to narrow down your searches.
